# Target Audience

CrystalOS is for people who **notice**. It is designed for those who appreciate the finer details of software, speed, privacy, and beauty.

## Primary Users

These are the users who will immediately see the value in CrystalOS and are likely to switch platforms.

| Type | Why They'll Switch |
|------|-------------------|
| **Designers** | For the beauty. The cursor alone will convert them. |
| **Developers** | For the speed, the agent architecture, and the built-in IDE. |
| **Students** | For the price ($19) and the inspiration to build. |
| **Minimalists** | For the lack of bloat, ads, and tracking. |
| **Privacy Advocates** | For the no-telemetry, no-data-selling promise. |
| **Old Hardware Users** | Because it runs beautifully on 5+ year old machines. |
| **MIT Professors** | For the intellectual curiosity in the agent-based OS architecture. |

## Secondary Users

These users might adopt CrystalOS as it grows, influenced by its initial success or specific use cases.

| Type | Why They Might Come |
|------|---------------------|
| **General Consumers** | If they see it on a friend's laptop and fall in love with the experience. |
| **Gamers** | Once game developers begin targeting CrystalOS with native ports. |
| **Enterprise** | If the agent architecture proves to be highly secure and manageable at scale. |
| **Education** | If schools adopt it due to the low cost, speed, and simplicity. |

## Who Will Never Switch

CrystalOS is an opinionated piece of software. It is not designed to be everything for everyone. We acknowledge that the following users are unlikely to adopt our platform:

*   People deeply invested in the Windows or macOS ecosystems.
*   Gamers who require specific, Windows-only titles.
*   Companies that rely on legacy software requirements.
*   Users who are afraid of learning something new.

And that's okay. CrystalOS isn't for everyone. It's a "rebellion against bloat, ads, and indifference," meant for those who resonate with that message.
